Trademark Filing & application

Registering a trademark is important for securing your brand. We deal with the entire process seamlessly, eliminating surprises and covering all jurisdictions you require.

What Is Trademark Filing?

Trademark filing has implications on legal rights of the brand. In this process, one is required to write an application to the competent government department. The application must provide details regarding your trademark such as its graphic, words, and the goods or services it seeks protection of.

Why Is Trademark Filing Important?
  1. Legal Protection: Trademark registration provides one with exclusive propriety to use the mark on products or the services an organization deals with. It prevents other competitors or companies from tendering a similar mark that might be deceitful to the consumers or lessen the strength of your brand.
  2. Brand Identity: A registered trademark enhances the image of your brand making it easier for consumers to identify and incidently patronize your products or services.
  3. Market Advantage: An organization is privileged when it gets a registered trademark because it merely situates its brand from others within the market. This can mean the difference between creating a brand and strengthening the position for consumers and the end of consumers’ loyalty.
The Trademark Filing Process
  1. Trademark Search: To obtain your rights and before filing the papers, follow a thorough search. This assists you in identifying if your trademark is unique and does not infringe on the trademark of another person.
  2. Application Preparation: Prepare an application for your trademark. It should have all the necessary information such as the design of your trademark, its name and the goods/services for which it is to be used.
  3. Filing the Application: Submit your application to the right trademark registry.
  4. Examination: we will take a look at your application and make sure it meets all the legal rules and doesn’t clash with any existing trademarks.
  5. Publication: The application goes public for opposition if it passes examination. This allows other parties to contest the registration if they think it violates their rights.
  6. Registration: Your trademark gets legal protection through registration if no one objects or if all challenges are settled.

Understanding Trademark Opposition

Anti-proposal is a legal procedure in which the third party contends with the registration of the mark before it is registered. On publication of your trademark application in the trademark office public gazette, there is a period of between 30 and 90 days when others can oppose the application.

The Basic Grounds for a Trademark Opposition

Likelihood of Confusion: Critics can decline your trademark as well similar to their current trademark which may create confusion to the customers.

Descriptiveness: Trademark may be considered as being merely descriptive of a product or a service and not eligible for exclusive protection.

Bad Faith: However, where the application is made in what could be characterized as ‘bad faith’ – for instance, to seek to capitalize on the goodwill associated with a famous trademark – it can be challenged.

Prior Rights: The other party may state they have prior rights to the trademark or a similar mark in the relevant geographical locale.

Trademark Litigation

Trademark Litigation refers to the legal proceedings, which take place when there are contents over the ownership or use of a trademark. It is one of the most important aspects of the IP law which aims at safeguarding the market identity of brands so that the individuals cannot be confused any longer.

What is Trademark Litigation?

Trademark litigation means suing or being sued over the infringement of a particular trademark. Trademark infringement occurs when one party feels the other is using a trademark that is a too close imitation of theirs; they can proceed to a legal suit. It is a process that must be in place to preserve the singularity of the brand and the consumer is not confused.

Why Trademark Litigation Matters

Trademark litigation is important in cases where companies wish to safeguard the image of their brand and their position on the market. Through litigation, a brand owner can avoid cases of imitations of similar trademarks that may cause a blow in the value of the brand as well as confuse customers. An action that is likely to be granted in a court of law to offset the infringed trademark includes the cancellation of the offending party from using the disputed trademark, awarding damages or transfer of the trademark ownership among others.
